Using a Deep Learning Model to Predict Flash Flood Likelihood

By: Raneesh Adulla

Background

Flash floods are the most dangerous type of flood, causing devastating damage with little to no warning and costing billions of dollars annually in property damage and lives lost. Unlike other natural disasters, flash floods can occur anywhere with sudden heavy rainfall, overwhelming streets and communities in minutes. Areas near coastlines, densely populated cities, and flood-prone regions face the greatest risk, as the intensity and frequency of these events continue to rise worldwide.

Rationale

Because current detection methods rely on immediate rainfall measurements and weather imaging, they often provide little advance warning, leaving residents with minimal time to prepare. A more accurate and proactive prediction system is necessary to reduce the immense human and economic costs of flash floods, particularly for vulnerable populations living in high-risk areas. Leveraging AI models offers the potential to forecast floods earlier and more reliably, improving preparedness and safety.

Engineering Goal & Objectives

The engineering goal of this project is to design, build, and test a deep-learning model capable of predicting flash floods one month in advance using rainfall data from the previous twelve months. By employing artificial neural networks and logistic regression, the model aims to achieve at least 95% accuracy in classifying whether a flash flood will occur in the following month, enabling residents in flood-prone areas to make timely and informed decisions for evacuation and safety.
